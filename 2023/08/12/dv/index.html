DATA VISUALIZATION

Every data visualization project starts with a requirement, whether it comes from a problem or a decision. How do we visualize the massive data generated by a product?

Step 1: Understand the Business Problem

The focus is on understanding the overall scope of the work, business objectives, the information stakeholders seek, the type of analysis they want to use, and the key deliverables expected.

Step 2: Data Preparation

Identify key variables to study the data, look for errors, and clean the data correctly. In a business environment, involving individuals with keen knowledge of the source systems, like DBAs, can be helpful.

Step 3: Modeling

Use various modeling techniques to test data and find answers to given objectives. Common models include linear regression, decision trees, and random modeling.

Step 4: Validation

Thoroughly evaluate the model and review the steps taken to ensure it aligns with business objectives. Document issues and data quality challenges.

Step 5: Visualization

Data visualization is crucial for conveying results to clients. Clearly define business objectives, break the process into steps, and provide the best deliverables for the client.

Step 6: Documentation

Document the project, data sources, data overview and quality, limitations, transformations, models, and their impact or usefulness.

Additionally, before starting the project, involving the right participants is crucial. Business representatives’ involvement is essential for defining project requirements, and data users should be engaged when discussing data they manage.

Data visualization should be a highly iterative and dynamic process. As Jon Steel said, “The ability of planners to look at the same information as everyone else and see something different is priceless.” Data visualization is vital for helping users discover patterns and insights.
