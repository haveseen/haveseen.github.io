PATENT STRATEGY

“Patent strategy” reflects the quality of patents that determine a company’s ability to establish dominance in a relevant field, aiming for a favorable position of “quantity and quality” in market competition.

Technical Points

Select core technical themes based on a dynamic assessment of the company’s in-house research and development and market feedback on in-house products. List core technical elements, break them down by technical branches, identify several core technical features, and hierarchically explore the entire technical solution, ensuring that technical features are ultimately presented in the form of minimal units.

Patent Mining

From a patent perspective, analyze, dissect, filter, and reasonably speculate on the vast array of technological achievements. Extract valuable technological innovations and technical solutions that have patent application and protection value from the pool of innovative achievements.

Patent Cross-Referencing

Select the technology solutions employed and the achieved technological results, analyze potential technological shortcomings, and provide corresponding solutions.

Patent Avoidance

Conduct patent searches on core technical features, selecting several existing patents that are most similar to these features. Analyze the technical issues, improvements, and achieved technical results corresponding to these existing patents, and compile patent avoidance strategies.

Intellectual property is one of a company’s crucial assets, and through patent strategy, research and development achievements can effectively be transformed into patent assets, supporting the company’s competitive advantage in global product markets.
